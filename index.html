Courses

Advanced Manufacturing Technology

Advanced Manufacturing and Industrial Maintenance introduces students to manufacturing processes and the design, operation, and maintenance of modern production systems. Topics include safety, measurement, machining, electrical circuits, PLCs, mechanical, pneumatic, and hydraulic systems, plus automation and robotics. Learning combines classroom instruction, lab work, and self-directed projects for hands-on experience.

Architecture & Engineering Technology

Architecture & Engineering Design Technology prepares students for university programs in architecture, engineering, interior design, and construction management. Students study design concepts for residential and commercial structures, mechanical design, CAD software (AutoCAD, Revit, SolidWorks), construction methods, surveying, and CNC basics. The first year explores both architecture and engineering, while the second year allows specialization in architectural design, mechanical design, or digital electronics.

Automotive Collision Repair Technology

Automotive Collision Repair Technology teaches juniors and seniors the skills needed for all aspects of vehicle body repair. Students begin with minor repairs and painting, then advance to replacing sheet metal and repairing major collision damage. The program emphasizes technical skills, safety, leadership, and work ethics to prepare students for further education or careers in the collision repair industry.

Automotive Service Technology

Automotive Service Technology is a two-year ASE/NATEF-certified program that prepares students for entry-level positions or further training in the automotive industry. Students study seven key areas: brakes, steering and suspension, engine repair and performance, HVAC, electrical systems, and drivetrains. Training combines classroom instruction and lab work focused on diagnostics, repair, service software, parts acquisition, and repair order processing.

Building Construction Technology

Building Construction Technology combines classroom, lab, and jobsite experience to teach design, installation, maintenance, and repair of residential and commercial structures. Students practice estimating, measuring, cutting, fitting, and installing finish materials while strengthening math, reading, writing, and communication skills essential for the construction industry.

Computer Science

Computer Science introduces students to programming, software development, and core computing concepts such as logic, data structures, and cybersecurity. Students learn multiple languages—including Python, Java, SQL, and HTML/JS—while creating programs, websites, and apps. The course also explores data analytics, game design, and cloud computing, using tools like Android Studio, GitHub, VS Code, and Raspberry Pi to prepare students for technology-focused careers.

Criminal Justic/Public Safety

Criminal Justice/Public Safety introduces students to law enforcement, the U.S. criminal justice system, and courtroom procedures through classroom study and hands-on activities. Students practice police operations, learn about cultural awareness, and explore criminology and criminal investigation. Advanced training includes processing mock crime scenes, collecting evidence, and applying real-world forensic techniques.

Culinary Arts

Culinary Arts prepares students for careers and further education in food service, nutrition, and hospitality. Through classroom and lab experience, students learn food preparation, safety, sanitation, and presentation while managing kitchen operations and equipment. The program also covers nutrition, menu planning, and meeting special dietary needs in professional culinary settings.

Cybersecurity

Cybersecurity teaches students the fundamentals of information and data protection, including firewalls, VPNs, and security planning. Topics cover network attacks, malware, wireless, email, and web security, as well as disaster recovery. Students also explore managerial aspects like access control, governance, policy development, and ethical practices to prepare for careers in information security.

Diesel Service Technology

Diesel Service Technology is a two-year ASE/NATEF-accredited program for juniors and seniors, covering diesel engines, drivetrains, brakes, electrical systems, HVAC, suspension, steering, and preventive maintenance. Students train in both classroom and lab using industry-standard tools, equipment, and factory service information to gain hands-on experience with all vehicle systems.

Electrical Service Technology

Electrical Service Technology provides classroom and lab training in residential, commercial, and industrial wiring, as well as electric motors and controls, with a strong focus on safety. Students learn electricity generation, system installation, maintenance, and planning, following NCCER curriculum and Electrical Training Alliance standards, while applying theory through hands-on experiences.

Grapic Communications/Digital Media

Graphic Communications/Digital Media teaches students to create and manipulate computer graphics for visual communication. Using industry-standard vector and layout software, students learn digital imaging, scanning, image capture, processing, and output techniques, gaining hands-on experience with modern graphic technologies.

Health Sciences: Exercise Science

Health Science: Exercise Science teaches students anatomy, physiology, and how the body responds to physical activity and athletic performance. Topics include energy systems, cardiovascular, respiratory, nervous, endocrine, skeletal, and muscular systems, plus nutrition and wellness. Students also learn medical terminology, earn CPR/AED certification, complete clinical observations, and participate in work-based learning to prepare for college and careers in health and fitness fields.

Health Sciences: Pre-Professional

Health Science: Pre-Professional is a two-year program providing a foundation in anatomy, physiology, medical terminology, and healthcare careers. Students learn patient care, professionalism, and communication skills while exploring labs in forensics, imaging, dental, cardiac, and more. Certifications include CPR/AED, CCMA, HIPAA, and OSHA 10, with clinical observations in two healthcare areas to prepare for college and careers.

HVAC-R & Energy Systems

HVAC-R & Energy Systems teaches students the design, installation, operation, and troubleshooting of heating, ventilation, air conditioning, and refrigeration systems. Coursework includes blueprint reading, technical manuals, system controls, and hands-on fabrication of sheet metal components, preparing students for residential and commercial HVAC-R careers.

Media Communications & Broadcasting

Media Communication & Broadcasting prepares students for careers in broadcasting, journalism, public relations, marketing, media production, and communications. Students develop skills in writing, speaking, media production, and technical functions, including hands-on on-air training at EVSC radio station 90.7 WPSR, while learning to operate in real-world business environments.

Precision Machine Metalworking Tehnology

Precision Machine Metalworking teaches students to shape and fabricate metal parts using lathes, drill presses, grinders, mills, and bench work. Instruction covers the use, care, and measurement techniques of precision tools like micrometers, indicators, calipers, and combination squares through hands-on classroom and lab experiences.

Veterinary Science

Veterinary Science introduces students to the field of veterinary care for livestock and companion animals. Students gain hands-on experience in anatomy, physiology, genetics, reproduction, nutrition, animal health, and veterinary office management, while learning skills required for veterinary assistant roles and proper animal care and maintenance.

Welding Technology

Welding Technology teaches students welding skills following American Welding Society (AWS) standards, including geometry, precision measurement, and estimation. The program covers metallurgy, gases, and materials science through classroom and hands-on lab experiences, preparing students for industry certifications and careers in welding.
