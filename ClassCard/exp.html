Advanced Manufacturing and Industrial Maintenance is designed to provide students with a basic understanding of manufacturing processes and the principles and practices associated with the design, operation, and maintenance of those processes. Areas of study include; safety, measurement, layout, machining, manufacturing processes, electrical circuits, programmable logic controllers, mechanical systems, pneumatic systems, hydraulic systems, automation and robotics. This course will utilize a combination of classroom, lab, and self-directed learning activities to provide students with a basic understanding and application of the subject matter.

Architectural Design is a dynamic course that immerses students in the art and science of creating functional and aesthetically pleasing structures. Students will learn the fundamental principles of architectural drawing, design concepts, and building codes. Through hands-on projects and software applications, they will develop skills in sketching, rendering, and computer-aided design (CAD) to bring their architectural visions to life. The curriculum covers various architectural styles, sustainable design practices, and the historical context of architecture.

Construction Technology provides hands-on experience in various aspects of the construction industry. Students will learn about building materials, carpentry, masonry, plumbing, and electrical systems. The course emphasizes safety protocols, blueprint reading, and modern construction techniques, preparing students for entry-level positions or further education in trades.

Robotics Engineering explores the design, construction, operation, and application of robots. Students will learn about mechanical systems, electronics, programming, and artificial intelligence. Through project-based learning, they will build and program robots to perform various tasks, gaining skills essential for careers in automation and advanced technology.
